### The Website
This website was created as part of a web development project for the course “Web-Entwicklung (Agentic Coding. LLM-supported web development in practice)” at the University of Graz (Austria).

**Code:**
The entire codebase was generated using AI, based on instructions provided by me.

**Content:**
- **Texts:** The texts were generated using Artificial Intelligence, which was provided in advance with the websites from which to extract information. All the websites used to generate the content are listed in the “sources” section of each page.
- **Quotations:** Passages from the Divine Comedy were added manually using the following digitized versions available on Wikisource:
 - Italian: _La Commedia secondo l'antica vulgata_, edited by Giorgio Petrocchi, Casa Editrice Le Lettere, Florence, 1994
- English: _The Divine Comedy_, translation by Henry Wadsworth Longfellow, 1867
- German: _Dante Alighieris Göttliche Komödie._ Translated and annotated by Karl Streckfuß. Revised edition with updated translation and commentary by Dr. Rudolf Pfleiderer. Leipzig: Philipp Reclam Jr., 1876
    The source for each quoted passage from the Divine Comedy is indicated in a dedicated paragraph at the end of each page.
- Translations: The English and German translations were generated by Artificial Intelligence based on the original Italian text.

**Disclaimer:**
The purpose of this project is to develop skills in web development, not to provide detailed or historically accurate information about Dante or the history of the places mentioned.
Although efforts were made to rely on official sources and websites, historical accounts of Dante’s life are varied, sometimes contradictory, and often incomplete.
For this reason, the use of this website for academic or historical research is strongly discouraged; readers are encouraged to consult more reliable sources.
