## Bologna

### Introduction

During his exile, Dante Alighieri also spent time in Bologna, one of the most influential cities of medieval Italy, renowned for its university and vibrant cultural and legal life.  
Details about Dante’s stay in Bologna remain uncertain: historians cannot pinpoint exactly when or for how long he resided there. However, it is widely accepted that he frequented the city between the late 13th and early 14th centuries, likely to study and engage in cultural exchanges.  
Bologna plays a notable role in Dante’s work, not so much for documented biographical events, but for the numerous references in the *Divina Commedia*, which reveal his intimate knowledge of the city and its political and social environment.

#### Places associated with Dante

- _Torre della Garisenda_
- _Basilica di San Domenico_
- Former Convent of the _Frati Gaudenti_

### Torre della Garisenda

Built in the 12th century and standing about 48 meters tall today, the _Torre della Garisenda_ is one of Bologna’s iconic “Two Towers.” Its distinctive tilt, caused by structural problems already present in medieval times, made it an unmistakable landmark in the city skyline.  
Dante explicitly references it in the *Divina Commedia*, using the tower as a visual comparison for the giant _Anteo_:

_"As seems the Carisenda, to behold_  
_Beneath the leaning side, when goes a cloud_  
_Above it so that opposite it hangs;_<br><br>
_Such did Antæus seem to me, who stood_  
_Watching to see him stoop, and then it was_  
_I could have wished to go some other way._  
Divina Commedia, Inferno, XXXI, 136-138

### Basilica of San Domenico

The _Basilica di San Domenico_ is one of the main religious landmarks in Bologna, built starting in the 13th century and closely associated with _Domenico di Guzmán_, founder of the Dominican Order, who is buried here.  
Inside the basilica, a plaque displays verses from the *Paradiso* dedicated to the saint:

_"Dominic was he called; and him I speak of_  
_Even as of the husbandman whom Christ_  
_Elected to his garden to assist him._  
Divina Commedia, Paradiso, XII, 70-72



These lines appear in the sphere of the Sun, where Dante encounters the wise spirits, including _San Bonaventura_, who recounts the life of _San Domenico_.

### Former Convent of the _Frati Gaudenti_

At number 40 _Via Arienti_, a plaque marks the site of the former convent of the so-called _Frati Gaudenti_, members of the Order of the Knights of the Blessed Virgin Mary Glorious, founded in the 13th century in Bologna to maintain civil peace.  
Dante places them among the hypocrites in the *Inferno*, criticizing their moral corruption:

_"Frati Gaudenti were we, and Bolognese;_  
_I Catalano, and he Loderingo_  
_Named, and together taken by thy city,_  
_As the wont is to take one man alone,_<br><br>
_For maintenance of its peace; and we were   such_  
_That still it is apparent round Gardingo."_  
Divina Commedia, Inferno, XXIII, 103-108
