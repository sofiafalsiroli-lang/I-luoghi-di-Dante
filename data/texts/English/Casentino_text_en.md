# Casentino

## Introduction
During his exile, Dante Alighieri found refuge in the Casentino, a valley in the Tuscan Apennines, hosted by the noble _Guidi_ family, who controlled the area and managed numerous castles and villages.  
It is likely that Dante stayed in several locations across the Casentino, moving between different castles and residences of the _Guidi_ family.  
In this territory, Dante engaged in political mediation, interacting with local lords and navigating tensions between Guelphs and Ghibellines—experiences that would later influence some of his political reflections in the _Divina Commedia_.

## Places associated with Dante
- Poppi Castle
- Romena Castle

<h3 id="ca-1-castello-poppi">Poppi Castle</h3>
The Poppi Castle, built in the 13th century by the Conti Guidi, dominates the village and its main square.  
Dante probably stayed here during his exile, as suggested by local sources and historical chronicles.  
The village and the castle represent a typical example of a medieval fortified center, serving both defensive and administrative purposes.


<h3 id="ca-2-castello-romena">Romena Castle</h3>
The Romena Castle, built in the 12th century by the Conti Guidi, overlooks the village and the Casentino valley. According to tradition, Dante Alighieri stayed here during his exile.  
The territory is also associated with the character Mastro Adamo, whom Dante places in the Inferno, where he is punished as a coin forger.
